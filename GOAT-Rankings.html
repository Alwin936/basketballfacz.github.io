The Greatest Players of All Time


15. Jerry West

Even if you don't recognize Jerry West's name, you'll recognize his silhouette. Jerry West, often referred to as "the logo" is one of the greatest players in NBA history. He had a deadly jumpshot, with a lightning-quick release, making him the go to option in clutch situations. While many players have been slapped with the title "Mr. Clutch", Jerry West was the true embodiment of this name. Perhaps his most notable clutch shot was a 60 foot dagger that tied Game 3 of the NBA Finals versus the New York Knicks.

Apart from his clutch moments, West was also an All-Star every year of his career. During his 14-year tenure, he lead his Lakers to the finals 9 times, and was the only player in NBA history to win Finals MVP while being on the losing team. To add icing on the cake, Jerry West was the 3rd player in NBA history to score over 25,000 points, just behind Wilt Chamberlain and Oscar Roberston (the latter of which just barely missed this list). While some would argue that Elgin Baylor deserves this spot more, claiming that Elgin is technically a better player, There is a difference between the greatest and the best, so this spot undoubtedly goes to Jerry West.

14. Julius Erving

Julius Erving was the blue print for a lot of our favorite players that we all love. Dr. J was so good that he was Michael Jordan's idol. Yes, you didn't read that wrong. This guy was so good that arguably the greatest player of all time idolized him. In fact, Erving (who started his career in the ABA) has scored a combined 30,026 points across the NBA and the ABA. He was one of the pivotal pieces in the NBA-ABA merger in 1976. Without counting his ABA accomplishments, Erving made 5 All-NBA First Teams. While that doesn't sound like much, remember that he only spent 11 years in the NBA.

To add to his achievements, Erving won 3 ABA championships, three consecutive ABA MVPs, 5 All-ABA team nods, made 5 ABA All-Star games, and won the ABA scoring title three times. To prove that his prowess wasn't just because of a weaker league, he went and led his 76ers to the NBA title in the 1982-83 season. He also added an NBA MVP to his trophy case, 7 All-NBA nods, and made the NBA All-Star game 11 times. Julius Erving was Michael Jordan before Michael Jordan.

13. Hakeem Olajuwon

Hakeem Olajuwon is often hailed as the best interior defender in NBA history. He was also a great rebounder, averaging 11.5 rebounds a game and 3.1 blocks a game for his career. His main competitors in those categories are Moses Malone(often hailed as the greatest rebounder in NBA history, who just finished outside this list) and Dikembe Mutombo, who is tied with Ben Wallace for the most DPOYs in NBA history (4).

Olajuwon also lead his Rockets to two championships in 1994 and 1995. He did this in a wide open league, with the absence of Michael Jordan. These two championships are considered two of the most valuable championships in NBA history, behind only the Maverick's 2011 championship. It'd be a shame to make a list like this without arguably the best interior defender of all time.

11. Steph Curry

Steph Curry revolutionized how basketball is played. Before him, the three point line was more of something that just existed, a shot mainly to be taken only when the game was on the line and it was the only shot you could get. A big-man shooting threes back then was almost forbidden. And then the greatest shooter of all time showed up and changed that. Steph Curry set in motion the pace and space era of NBA basketball, and scoring has skyrocketed. Defenses are all focused on stopping the three, and there's no one better at making them than Steph Curry.

Defenses will hunt curry down all across the court like he's a wanted criminal, only for him to pull up from 30 feet, and you might as well add 3 points to the scoreboard, because you know it's gonna make it before it even leaves his hands. Curry combined with Klay Thompson to make the splash brothers, and they've won 4 championships together two far. They dominated in the latter half of the 2010s, setting the record for best record in the regular season (73-9). They pretty much ran through the league in 2017 and 2018 (with the addition of Kevin Durant), which would both have been won in sweeps if it wasn't for Lebron and Kyrie. Curry made it back to the finals in 2022, and beat the Celtics almost against all odds, winning Finals MVP in the process. Thanks to the anomaly of Stephen Curry, basketball will never look the same again.

9. Tim Duncan

Tim Duncan was one of the most humble players in NBA history. His playstyle was simple, yet way more effective than it looked like it should have been. His Spurs were boring to watch, none of their players scored a ton, and there was no clear-cut star. Yet, the Spurs dynasty still won 5 rings, never making the finals back-to-back until the 2012-13 and 2013-14 seasons. Tim Duncan made great use of the bank shot, which was his main method of scoring jumpers. He was never selfish, and always looked out for his teammates.

Tim Duncan also had a lot of accomplishments as a player, He won Finals MVP for two of the aforementioned rings, two regular season MVPs, 15 All-NBA selections, and 15 All-star selections. On top of that, he has the most All-Defensive team selections of any NBA player in history, with 15. All of these add up to give Tim Duncan a well-rounded career, accolade wise. No wonder Tim Duncan is often hailed as the greatest power forward of all time.

8. Wilt Chamberlain

Wilt Chamberlain is the second most dominant player in NBA history. His record of scoring 100 points in a game, way back in 1962, still stands to this day. In fact, closest anyone has ever came is 81 points. In that same 1961-1962 season, Wilt also averaged 50.4 points, 25.6 rebounds, 2.4 assists, with his steals and blocks not recorded, but weere likely huge numbers. He won Rookie of the Year, averaging 37.6 points, 27.0 rebounds, and 2.3 assists a game. These are by far the best stats by a rookie to this day.

Wilt also won two championships, winning the Finals MVP for one of them. He made the also made the All-Star team 13 times in his 14 year career. In his storied career, he also won 4 MVP awards. Wilt looked pretty skinny, and was super tall, so he earned the nickname "Wilt the Stilt". Not forgetting that he still holds the record for total rebounds in an NBA career (23,924). He also met his main adversary, Bill Russell, several times in the finals, losing all but one. If it wasn't for the dominance of the 1960s Celtics dynasty, Wilt very well might have had 5 or more championships. Wilt Chamberlain was a 7'1" basketball demigod in a pair of converse sneakers.

7. Larry Bird

Larry Bird is second to only Bill Russell in terms of the greatest Celtics. He never got much hype, but came into the NBA, and set everything on fire. His rivalry with Magic Johnson is second to none. Larry would insult you, your style of play, and your family the night before he played you, and then proceed to drop 40 on you the next day. In fact, Larry won three straight three-point contests, winning without taking a warm-up in 1988. Two years, later Craig Hodges won a three-point contest Larry didn't participate in, and said "[Larry] knows where to find me". Larry when hearing so replied "Yeah, at the end of the bulls bench." His trash talk, and how he backed it up gave him the nickname "Larry Legend."

Larry Bird once decided to play a game with only his left hand, to prove that he wasn't strong hand dominant. It didn't matter that Larry was using his weaker hand. He finished the game with 47 points, 14 rebounds, and 11 assists in a 120-119 overtime victory. Sadly, he suffered an injury while taking out the trash, and he was never quite the same. He had to stretch for two hours just to run, but he still suited up. Bird ended his career with 3 championships, 3 MVPs, and 2 Finals MVPs. Never disrespect Larry Legend.

6. Magic Johnson

In the Magic-Bird rivalry, Magic was the slightly more successful one. He won 5 championships, 3 MVPs, 3 Finals MVPs, and made an All-NBA team 10 times. He also had 12 All-Star selections, and 9 All-Defensive selections. At 6'9", Magic Johnson is stil the tallest day-to-day point-gaurd the NBA has ever seen. He was always rivals with Bird, even back in College. Even in the NCAA championships, it all boiled down to Magic vs. Bird. While Magic wasn't quite as good as Bird at shooting, he made up with it through passing.

Magic is arguable the greatest passer of all time. He averaged 11.2 assists a game in his career, and led the league in assists 4 times. Magic made a shot dubbed the "baby skyhook", with 4 seconds left of the 1987 NBA finals, winning game 4 for LA, and putting them up for good. Magic's passing mastery was so overwhelming that there was no true way to stop him. Leave him open, and he's going to score 50 points. Double-team him, and he's going to assist 50 points. Magic Johnson wasn't just a player. He was a phenomenon that transcended generations.

5. Bill Russell

Bill Russell is perhaps the most accomplished player in NBA history. He was the head of the missile that was the 60s dynasty Celtics. He won a total of 11 championships in his career, along with his right-hand man, John Havlicek who won 7 rings. Bill and John absolutely WALKED the league in the 60s, winning 8 championships in a row from 1959-1966. He also absolutely dominated the league in rebounding, second only to his main rival, Wilt Chamberlain. Bill and Wilt met several times in the finals, and due to the fact that Bill generally had a better team around him, he won most of the times.

Sadly, the Finals MVP wasn't a thing when Bill Russell won his championships, but if it was, he would likely have the most of that kind in NBA history. Bill Russell was also a demon defensively. I've already mentioned his rebounding prowess, but he was also great at playing interior defense and converting defense to offense. Russell also found success outside of being a player, and was a great coach. Bill Russell's record of 11 championships will likely never be broken.

4. Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant was a true legend. The black mamba was known for his insane worth ethic, larger than life ego, and unmatched mentality. He was known to be ruthless, even to his teammates. When they finally convinced him to come with them to clubs to party late at night, he gave the asterisk that he'd still be there waking them up at 6 in the morning. That made them realize that they either need to match Kobe's energy, or they had to stop this. Kobe was also never satisfied, even when he was up in the playoffs. A perfect sum of how he felt is his famous quote "[the] job's not done".

Kobe once stopped shooting in a crucial game 7, to prove that the Lakers couldn't win without him, and that they needed to get him help. This ended up resulting in a trade for Pau Gasol, and Kobe proved he could win without Shaq. Kobe also scored the closest to Wilt Chamberlain's 100 point record in a game (81), and has the most points of any player in his final game ever (60). Kobe ended up winning 5 championships, 15 All-NBA teams, 18 All-Star teams, and 12 All-Defensive teams (the most of any gaurd in NBA history). Sadly, Kobe died on Janruary 26, 2020, in a helicopter crash, at 41 years old. Never forget the black mamba.

3. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

If it wasn't for the human known as Lebron James, Kareem's scoring record might have held forever. He scored 38,387 points in his career thanks to extreme consistency. His signature move, the skyhook, was unstoppable, especially when you factored in Kareem's 7'1" tree-trunk frame. He was skinny, yet extremely tall and solid, and there was nothing that could realisticly be done to stop him. In fact, opponents clawed at his eyes so much in order to stop him, that he had to wear goggles later in his career to protect his eyes.

Kareem was very hyped, and lived up to it. He led his Bucks to their first ever title in 1971. After a few more years with the bucks, Kareem went to play with the Lakers. He joined up with Magic Johnson, and later James Worthy, to create the "show time lakers", who were very successful. In total, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar won 6 NBA championships, 6 regular season MVPs (the most in NBA history), and 2 Finals MVPs. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar was the apex of legends.
